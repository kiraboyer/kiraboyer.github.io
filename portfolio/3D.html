Time

Kira Boyer, Time, video, 00:00:34. 2018.

Time moves quickly, seemingly at the blink of an eye. Days lapse into nights and we age as fast as the Earth orbits. Long after our generations are gone, the mountains and oceans will continue to grow for billions of years. The man and woman sit, passively, and observe the passing of time. In the short 34 second time span of the video, they age from children, to adults, to elders, until they are no longer visible. Time is linear, always moving forward, and humans will live on Earth and pass away in the blink of an eye.

Underwater

Kira Boyer, Under Water, video, 00:00:40. 2018.

As someone with a diagnosed general anxiety disorder, I often find myself dealing with irrational, debilitating episodes of symptoms that leave me too weak to do anything. Most people with this disorder seek medicine and years of therapy. I find art to be my own form of therapy. Through this self portrait animation, I capture the essence of what an anxiety attack feels and looks like. In this virtual space, am free to break physical boundaries to exhibit this attack in a dramatized fashion that even those with anxiety can relate to. The figure is grayscale and lacks any vibrancy, she folds herself into a fetal position to close herself off from her surroundings, and a body of water with aggressive waves surrounds her. This animation mimics the irrational fear, pressure, depression, shortness of breath, and rapid heart rate of an anxiety attack.

Casino Dreamscape

Kira Boyer, Casino Dreamscape, 3D Render, 960x540px. 2018.

The intermingling smells of cigarette smoke and liquor, the sounds of a hundred slot machines playing at once, the bright neon lights and tacky carpets, and the local live jazz band playing for a bar. This makes up the aesthetic of a casino, a place designed to capture your attention and hold it until you’ve spent and lost more money than you came in with. Growing up around casinos, I’ve always been fascinated with the techniques that casino designers will use to keep people in one place, thoughtlessly throwing money into machines which, more times than not, will only return to them disappointment, hope, and flashing, bright neon lights. I hope to capture the essence of a 70’s casino dreamscape; I aim for the viewer to feel melancholy in the emptiness of the casino installation for an era long gone, yet drawn to the enticing lights and colors.

Campfires, Vampire, Crime Scenes, Bees, and More (Art 103 Show)

Kira Boyer in collaboration with Lauren Chun, Kevin Nguyen, Kong Vue, and Sharon Faye, ...And Then There Were Bees, 3D Print, glue, wood, chicken wire, iPhone. 2018.

A conceptual piece pondering what life after humans will be like. What artifacts will we leave behind and what will the beings left behind make of these artifacts? We answer this by choosing to pursue a future in which bees only remain; a twist on the reality that bees are endangered. We presume that even our most valued objects (cell phones) would inevitably become a home to the bees.

Kira Boyer in collaboration with Vanessa More, Once Upon a Time, Wood, Cardboard, Paper. 2018.

This interactive storybook takes the symbols of storybook tropes and makes them buildable upon vines. The piece is split into two styles, Disney and The Brothers Grimm. My side, The Brothers Grimm, is dark and dangerous as the vines include sharp points and dark colors. The Disney side, by Vanessa Mora, is whimsical, rounded, and brightly colored. Both can be built up on the vines to tell a story and connect.
