The San Jose State University Digital Media Art program is proud to present DIMENSIONS, the Fall 2019 DMA BFA class exhibition. The event will take place in Gallery 5, Gallery 8, and the Black Gallery in the Art Building from November 12-18.

DIMENSIONS studies the relationship between human and machine, and how both interface with each other. This year’s exhibition will explore the multidimensional interfacing of society with technology. Some of the pieces will be an expression of personal experiences and perspectives through various media, while other installations will have more politically charged subjects, contributing to larger conversations. Themes explored include: identity, introspection, surveillance, time, memories, perception, and social media.

People are interfacing with machines and technology everyday and presentation or interaction with technology can affect the experience. DIMENSIONS consists of interdisciplinary and multimedia pieces of various installations of sculpture, video, sound, photography, web design, Arduino, and video games.
