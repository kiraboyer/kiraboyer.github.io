• Scoptophobia

    by Kira Boyer

    Artist Statement

    Kira Boyer is a digital media artist from the San Francisco bay area. Inspired by society and her experiences as she traverses through life, she uses digital software, sculptural techniques, drawing techniques, and conceptual processes to create her work. She intends to refer to the past to inform the present. No stranger to anxiety, Kira's work, Scoptophobia, addresses privacy in the digital age in the context of people's daily interactions with, or proximity to, their smart devices. Society surround themselves with technology and grant devices permission to exist as extensions of self within private and public spaces. These devices are always watching, in a way, whether they are tracking locations, being granted access to cameras and photos, collecting and selling personal data, etc. In this installation, the audience looking through the keyholes act as the voyeuristic cameras of devices looking into someone's personal world.

    Process

    Keyholes: The keyholes are first rendered as 3D models, then they are 3D printed. Once printed they are then painted with a metallic gold acrylic paint. After they have dried, a resin and bronze powder mixture is poured onto the faces of the keyholes. Once solidified, charcoal powder is rubbed into the grooves between the resin and raised gold details. One more coat of gold paint is dry brushed over the charcoal and the keyhole is sprayed with a fixative.

    The wooden encasings are laser cut, sanded, and stained. A space for the media players and their chords is also cut into the wood layers. The layers are glued together and fitted with hinges for easy media player handling as well as a door-like appearance. The media players are placed inside these encasings with black colored paper over them to hide anything that is not the media screen.

    Media: All media is filmed in the span of 1 week with a phone's front-facing camera.

    About

    Kira Boyer

    Born in 1997

    Lives in San Jose, CA, USA

    Kira Boyer began creating digital art in high school. In 2015 she enrolled at San Jose State University where she is now studying the interdisciplinary Digital Media Art program. She is the former President of the CADRE Student Organization at San Jose State where she has participated in events such as Makerfaire (2018 & 2019) and Gamer Gala (2018), as well as held a number of class exhibitions. Outside of school, she has exhibited at the San Francisco Palace of Fine Arts for the Surfrider Foundation's Message in a Bottle 4. 3D modeling, sculpture, creative coding, videography and physical computing are her true passions, but Kira is also interested in projection mapping, graphic art, photography, prop-making, VR, AR, and screen-printing.
